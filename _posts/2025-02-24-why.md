*[CAD]: Computer Aided Design
*[CAM]: Computer Aided Machining

Because very few people have patience to ask why in depth. It might be not necessary when dealing with well known problems and the well known template solutions are acceptable. But with increasing automation technology, these 'template' solutions are not cost competitive.

One example is the advent of commodity 3D printing solutions. Where previously templated solutions required the use of standard parts and restrictive fabrication methods, you could let your imagination run wild (for the most part) and the machine would slowly but surely create the part for you without futher human intervention other than to CAD/CAM the part out.

But to advance beyond the state of the art you have do more than that. A design merely transplanted from the old but just pushed into a new fabrication process can only provide limited gains. You would have to develop new design guides, techniques and question all the previous assumptions and rules of thumb.

So what's the problem?
